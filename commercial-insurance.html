Commercial Insurance

The foundation of your insurance program. Business owners policy, general liability, workers compensation, employment practices, commercial auto, and umbrella coverage for technology companies.

Standard commercial coverage

Every technology company needs a base of standard commercial insurance. These are the policies that cover your physical operations, your employees, and your general liability to third parties.

Business Owners Policy (BOP) / General Liability

Combines commercial property and general liability into one policy. Covers your office space, equipment, and liability for bodily injury or property damage to third parties. For technology companies that operate primarily in the cloud, the property component may be modest, but the general liability coverage is essential.

Workers Compensation

Required in most states once you have employees. Covers medical expenses and lost wages for employees injured on the job. Even for technology companies where the work is primarily desk-based, workers compensation is a legal requirement and protects both the company and the employees.

Employment Practices Liability (EPLI)

Covers claims from employees alleging wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, or retaliation. As your team grows, EPLI becomes increasingly important. These claims are among the most common and expensive for growing companies.

Commercial Auto

If your company owns or leases vehicles, or if employees drive their personal vehicles for work purposes, commercial auto coverage protects against liability and physical damage.

Umbrella / Excess Liability

Provides additional limits above your primary policies (GL, auto, EPLI). When a single claim exceeds your primary policy limits, the umbrella kicks in. For technology companies with large client contracts, umbrella coverage is often required to meet contractual minimum limits.

How commercial insurance fits with specialty coverage

Commercial insurance is the foundation. Cyber, Tech E&O, and D&O are the specialty layers built on top. A complete insurance program for a technology company typically includes both. We handle the full program, not just the specialty lines, so everything works together.
