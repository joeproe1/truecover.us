Common Questions

Straight answers to the insurance questions business owners actually ask.

Getting started

What insurance does my business actually need?

It depends on what you do, where you operate, how many employees you have, and what your contracts require. Most businesses need general liability at a minimum. Beyond that, it depends on your specific situation. I'll tell you what you need and what you don't -- I'm not going to sell you coverage you don't need.

Do I really need more than the minimum?

Maybe, maybe not. The "minimum" is usually what your lease or a client contract requires. Whether you need more depends on your actual risk. A business with two employees and no client contracts has different needs than one with 20 employees and enterprise clients. I'll give you a straight answer based on your situation.

Why do different brokers give me different prices for the same coverage?

Because it's usually not the same coverage. Different carriers have different policy language, different exclusions, and different limits structure. A cheaper quote might have a lower sublimit on something important, or an exclusion that doesn't show up until you read the fine print. I compare what the policies actually say, not just the premium.

Claims and coverage

What happens when I file a claim?

You report the claim to your carrier (I can help with this). They assign an adjuster who investigates. Then they either pay, deny, or negotiate. The most common reason claims get denied is an exclusion the policyholder didn't know about. That's why I read the policy before you need to use it -- so there are no surprises.

Can my claim really get denied because of something in the fine print?

Yes. It happens more often than people think. An exclusion, a sublimit, a definition that doesn't match your operations -- these are all reasons carriers deny or reduce claims. The policy language is what matters, not the brochure or the proposal. I use AI to read every page and flag these issues before they become a problem.

What's the difference between cyber insurance and professional liability?

Professional liability (E&O) covers claims from your clients when your work or advice causes them financial harm. Cyber insurance covers your own losses from security incidents -- data breaches, ransomware, system outages. A client suing you because your work failed is E&O. Your business losing money because your systems got hacked is cyber. Different problems, different policies.
