Common Questions

Answers to the insurance questions technology companies ask most.

Cyber Insurance

Does my cyber policy cover ransomware payments?

Most cyber policies include ransomware coverage, but the details vary. Some require prior written consent before payment. Some apply sublimits. Some include OFAC sanctions exclusions that can void coverage entirely. The answer depends on your specific policy language, not the marketing materials.

Do I need cyber insurance if I already have Tech E&O?

Yes. Tech E&O covers claims from clients when your product or service fails. Cyber insurance covers your own losses from security incidents. A client suing you because your software had a vulnerability is a Tech E&O claim. Your company losing revenue because your systems were encrypted is a cyber claim. Different exposures.

How much cyber insurance does a technology company need?

It depends on your revenue, the data you handle, your client contracts, and your regulatory environment. Client contracts often specify minimum limits ($1M, $3M, $5M). Start there and adjust based on your actual exposure. I can help you right-size the coverage without overpaying.

Technology E&O

Is Tech E&O the same as cyber insurance?

No. Tech E&O covers claims from your clients when your product or service fails. Cyber covers your own losses from security incidents. You likely need both. Some carriers offer combined policies, but a combined policy isn't always better than separate policies.

Why does the "professional services" definition matter so much?

Because it determines whether the policy responds to a claim. If you do managed security services but the policy only covers "IT consulting and support," the carrier can argue your security work isn't covered. This is the most common reason Tech E&O claims get disputed.

Do my client contracts affect what Tech E&O coverage I need?

Yes. Client contracts often include indemnification clauses, insurance requirements, and liability caps that directly affect what coverage you need. I read your contracts alongside your policy to identify mismatches before they become claim disputes.

Directors & Officers

When do I need D&O insurance?

If you have outside investors, a board of directors, or significant revenue, you should have D&O. Most companies buy it at Series A when investors require it. But even bootstrapped companies with employees and customers face management liability risk.

How much D&O coverage does a startup need?

Investor term sheets typically require $3-5 million in limits. The right amount depends on your stage, industry, regulatory exposure, and cap table. I can help you benchmark against similar companies.

What is the difference between D&O and EPLI?

D&O covers claims against management for decisions they made as company leaders. EPLI covers employment-related claims like wrongful termination, discrimination, and harassment. There is overlap, and some policies bundle them. Whether to buy separately or combined depends on your specific situation.
