Directors & Officers Insurance

Management liability for founders, board members, and company officers. Most institutional investors require D&O within 60-90 days of Series A close.

What it covers

  • Investor claims. Misrepresentation, breach of fiduciary duty, or mismanagement of company funds.
  • Regulatory investigations. Defense costs when a regulator investigates your business practices or compliance.
  • Employment claims. Wrongful termination, discrimination, or harassment allegations against management.
  • Customer or competitor claims. Unfair business practices, anti-competitive behavior, or misleading statements.
  • Securities claims. Claims related to equity offerings, valuation statements, or shareholder disputes.

D&O after fundraising

Most VC term sheets require D&O with minimum limits of $3-5 million. The requirement takes effect at close, giving the company 60-90 days to bind. This is not optional. Investors require it because they're joining your board and need personal protection.

If you're raising a Series A or B, D&O should be on your closing checklist alongside legal and banking.

What to watch for

  • Entity vs. individual coverage. Some policies only cover directors and officers personally. For private companies, entity coverage matters because the company is usually the target.
  • Regulatory investigation coverage. Not all D&O policies cover it. Critical for FinTech, health tech, and EdTech.
  • Prior acts. If you're buying D&O for the first time, prior acts coverage protects decisions made before inception. Without it, pre-policy claims are excluded.
  • Insured vs. insured exclusion. Prevents one insured from suing another. Can matter in co-founder disputes.
