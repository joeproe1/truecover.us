Technology Errors & Omissions Insurance

Professional liability for when your technology product or service causes a client financial harm. We make sure the policy covers what you actually do, not what the application assumed.

What Tech E&O covers

Technology Errors and Omissions insurance (Tech E&O) responds when a client claims they suffered a financial loss because of your technology product or professional service. This includes:

  • Software defects or failures. Your code has a bug that causes a client's system to go down or lose data.
  • Service delivery failures. You miss a deadline, misconfigure a system, or deliver work that does not meet the contracted specifications.
  • Negligent advice or recommendations. A client follows your technical recommendation and it causes them financial harm.
  • Intellectual property claims. Allegations that your product infringes on a patent, copyright, or trade secret.
  • Data loss or corruption. Your service causes a client to lose or corrupt their data.

The gap most brokers miss

Every Tech E&O policy includes a definition of "professional services" or "technology services." This definition controls what is covered. If the definition does not match what your company actually does, the policy does not respond when you need it.

This is the most common gap in Tech E&O coverage, and most brokers do not check it. They submit the application, get a quote, bind the policy, and never read the definition.

Examples of how this goes wrong:

  • IT services firm that also does security monitoring. The policy defines professional services as "IT consulting and support." The carrier argues that security monitoring is not consulting. Claim denied.
  • Software company that recommends third-party tools. The policy excludes liability arising from "products or services not developed by the insured." A client claims the third-party tool you recommended caused their loss. Not covered.
  • MSP that performs penetration testing. The policy has an intentional acts exclusion. The carrier argues that pen testing is intentional unauthorized access to a system. Coverage is disputed.

Tech E&O vs. General E&O

General professional liability (E&O) is designed for traditional professions: accountants, consultants, architects. Tech E&O is specifically designed for technology companies and includes coverage for technology-specific exposures like software failures, system downtime, and data loss.

If you are a technology company with a general E&O policy, your coverage may have significant gaps. The definitions, exclusions, and coverage triggers are different.

Common questions about Tech E&O

Is Tech E&O the same as cyber insurance?

No. Tech E&O covers claims from your clients when your product or service fails. Cyber insurance covers your own losses from security incidents. You likely need both. Some carriers offer combined policies, but the coverage details vary, and a combined policy is not always better than separate policies.

Why does the definition of "professional services" matter so much?

Because it determines whether the policy responds to a claim. If you do managed security services but the policy only covers "IT consulting and support," the carrier can argue your security work is not a covered service. This is the most common reason Tech E&O claims get disputed.

Do my client contracts affect what Tech E&O coverage I need?

Yes. Client contracts often include indemnification clauses, insurance requirements, and liability caps that directly affect what coverage you need. A broker who reads your contracts alongside your policy can identify mismatches before they become claim disputes.
