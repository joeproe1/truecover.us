About

Who I am

I'm Joerg Proeve. I've spent 20 years in insurance, but not as a typical broker. My career has been on the carrier and product side: underwriting, claims, system architecture, and building insurance products from scratch.

At Chubb and CNA, I worked on the systems that carriers use to price and manage policies. As COO of BOXX Insurance, I helped build a cyber insurance product from the ground up. Before insurance, I was an engineer at Siemens, working in cybersecurity.

That background means I understand what professional services firms face from both sides: the operational risks your practice carries, and how carriers write, price, and pay claims on the policies that cover those risks.

Why True Cover exists

Professional services firms get sold insurance by brokers who don't understand their operations. The policy says one thing. The practice does another. When a claim happens, the gap between the two is where coverage fails.

The accountant's E&O that defines "professional services" as "tax preparation" when you also do bookkeeping and advisory work. The law firm's cyber policy with a $50K sublimit on business interruption. The consultant's D&O that excludes regulatory investigations. The financial advisor's E&O that doesn't cover the new services added last year.

These aren't edge cases. They're the norm. And most brokers never catch them because they never read the policy.

The approach

I read the policy language. I cross-reference it against your firm's operations and contracts. I place coverage that matches what your practice does, not what the application said two years ago.

That's what True Cover means. Coverage you can count on because someone checked.
